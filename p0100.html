P0700

Transmission Control System Malfunction

Difficulty
Medium–High
Repair Time
1–4 hours
Cost
$150–$1500+
Severity
High

Why This Matters

The P0700 code in Audi vehicles indicates that the Transmission Control Module (TCM) has detected a fault and requested the Check Engine Light. This is not a standalone failure—it is a gateway code that signals deeper transmission issues. Ignoring it can lead to overheating, slipping gears, or total transmission failure, which is one of the most expensive repairs on a vehicle.

Symptoms

  • ✔ Check Engine Light and limp mode activation
  • ✔ Harsh, delayed, or erratic shifting behavior
  • ✔ Transmission stuck in a single gear
  • ✔ Reduced fuel efficiency due to improper gear ratios
  • ✔ Stalling or hesitation during acceleration

Causes

  • • Failed transmission solenoids or sensors
  • • Faulty or damaged Transmission Control Module (TCM)
  • • Wiring harness issues, shorts, or corrosion
  • • Low, dirty, or burnt transmission fluid
  • • Internal transmission mechanical damage

How to Fix

  • ✔ Retrieve and diagnose additional transmission codes
  • ✔ Replace faulty solenoids or sensors
  • ✔ Repair damaged wiring or connectors
  • ✔ Perform transmission fluid service
  • ✔ Replace or reprogram the TCM if necessary

Step-by-Step Diagnosis

  1. Scan for additional Audi/VW-specific transmission codes
  2. Check transmission fluid level and condition
  3. Inspect wiring harnesses and connectors for damage
  4. Test TCM power supply and ground circuits
  5. Evaluate solenoid and sensor performance
  6. Clear codes and perform road test

💡 Pro Tip

P0700 does not tell you what is broken—it only tells you where to look. Always use a scanner capable of accessing Audi transmission modules, or you risk misdiagnosing the issue and replacing the wrong parts.
