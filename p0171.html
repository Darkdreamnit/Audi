P0171

System Too Lean (Bank 1)

Difficulty
Medium
Repair Time
1–2 hours
Cost
$150–$600
Severity
Medium

Why This Matters

A lean condition means the engine is getting too much air or not enough fuel. If left unresolved, it can cause poor performance, engine damage, or overheating.

Symptoms

  • ✔ Check engine light
  • ✔ Rough idle
  • ✔ Poor acceleration
  • ✔ Engine hesitation

Causes

  • • Vacuum leak
  • • Dirty or faulty MAF sensor
  • • Weak fuel pump
  • • Clogged fuel injectors

How to Fix

  • ✔ Clean or replace MAF sensor
  • ✔ Repair vacuum leaks
  • ✔ Replace fuel filter
  • ✔ Inspect fuel system

💡 Pro Tip

Start by checking for vacuum leaks before replacing parts. Many P0171 issues are caused by cracked hoses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drive with P0171?

You can drive short distances, but the issue may worsen and cause engine damage if ignored.

What causes P0171?

Common causes include vacuum leaks, faulty MAF sensors, or fuel delivery issues.

How much does it cost to fix P0171?

Repair costs typically range from $150 to $600 depending on the root cause.
