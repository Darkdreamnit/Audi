P0299

Turbocharger Underboost Condition

Difficulty
Intermediate to Advanced
Repair Time
1–4 hours
Cost
$100–$2000+
Severity
High

Why This Matters

Underboost conditions significantly reduce engine performance and often indicate turbocharger issues or boost leaks. If ignored, this can lead to complete turbo failure and expensive repairs.

Symptoms

  • ✔ Significant loss of power
  • ✔ Limp mode activation
  • ✔ Check engine light
  • ✔ Hissing or whooshing noise
  • ✔ Weak or inconsistent turbo boost

Causes

  • • Worn or loose wastegate actuator (common on Audi 2.0T)
  • • Faulty diverter valve
  • • Boost leaks in hoses or intercooler piping
  • • Failing turbocharger
  • • Faulty boost pressure sensor

How to Fix

  • ✔ Repair or tighten wastegate linkage
  • ✔ Replace diverter valve
  • ✔ Fix boost leaks
  • ✔ Replace faulty sensor
  • ✔ Replace turbo if necessary

💡 Pro Tip

On Audi engines, the wastegate linkage is a very common cause of P0299. Even slight looseness can prevent proper boost and trigger this code.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drive with P0299?

You can drive short distances, but expect major power loss. Continued driving may damage the turbocharger.

What causes P0299?

Common causes include boost leaks, wastegate issues, diverter valve failure, or a worn turbocharger.

How much does it cost to fix P0299?

Repairs range from $100 for simple leaks up to $2000+ for turbo replacement.
