P0420

Catalytic Converter Efficiency Below Threshold (Bank 1)

Difficulty
Intermediate
Time
1–3 hrs
Cost
$200–$2000+
Severity
Medium

Why This Matters

The P0420 code means your catalytic converter is no longer efficiently reducing harmful emissions. While your Audi may still drive normally at first, this issue directly impacts emissions output and fuel efficiency. Over time, a failing catalytic converter can lead to reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and potential damage to other components. If ignored, it can also cause your vehicle to fail emissions testing and, in severe cases, restrict exhaust flow leading to noticeable power loss.

Symptoms

P0420 often starts off subtly, which is why many drivers ignore it at first. However, as the catalytic converter degrades further, performance issues can become more noticeable.

  • Check Engine Light: This is usually the only symptom early on, triggered when the ECU detects inefficient catalytic converter performance.
  • Reduced Engine Power: A clogged or failing converter can restrict exhaust flow, causing sluggish acceleration and poor throttle response.
  • Poor Fuel Economy: The engine may compensate for inefficiencies, leading to increased fuel consumption.
  • Exhaust Smell: In some cases, you may notice a sulfur or “rotten egg” smell from the exhaust.

Causes

While a failed catalytic converter is the most common cause of P0420, it’s important to understand that converters usually fail because of another underlying issue.

  • Failed Catalytic Converter: Over time, internal components break down due to heat, age, or contamination from oil or fuel.
  • Faulty Downstream O2 Sensor: The rear oxygen sensor may send incorrect readings, making the ECU think the converter is failing.
  • Exhaust Leaks: Leaks before or near the catalytic converter can introduce extra oxygen, skewing sensor readings.
  • Engine Problems: Misfires, rich fuel mixtures, or oil burning can damage the catalytic converter prematurely.

How to Fix (Step-by-Step)

Diagnosing P0420 correctly is critical because replacing a catalytic converter without fixing the root cause will result in the problem coming back.

  1. Check for Exhaust Leaks: Inspect the exhaust manifold, pipes, and connections for leaks that could affect sensor readings.
  2. Scan for Additional Codes: If you see misfire or fuel-related codes, fix those first before addressing P0420.
  3. Monitor O2 Sensor Data: Use a scan tool to compare upstream and downstream sensor readings. If they behave similarly, the converter is likely failing.
  4. Inspect the Catalytic Converter: Look for signs of clogging, overheating, or internal damage.
  5. Replace Faulty Components: Replace the catalytic converter or sensors as needed based on diagnosis.
  6. Clear Code and Test Drive: Verify the repair by clearing the code and monitoring system readiness.

💡 Pro Tip

Don’t replace the catalytic converter right away. Many P0420 cases are actually caused by failing oxygen sensors or small exhaust leaks. Always confirm with live data before committing to a costly repair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drive with P0420?
Yes, but long-term driving can reduce performance and damage the exhaust system.

Is P0420 serious?
It’s moderate. It won’t immediately break your car, but it should be fixed to avoid bigger issues.

How much does it cost to fix?
Costs range from $200 for sensors to over $2000 for a catalytic converter replacement.
