P0455

Evaporative Emission System Leak (Large Leak)

Difficulty
Beginner–Intermediate
Repair Time
10 min – 2 hrs
Cost
$0–$400
Severity
Low–Medium

Why This Matters

The P0455 code indicates a large leak in the EVAP system, meaning fuel vapors are escaping instead of being properly contained and recycled. While this usually doesn’t affect drivability, it will increase emissions and can prevent your Audi from passing inspection. In some cases, it can also lead to fuel odors or rough starting after refueling.

Symptoms

✔ Check engine light illuminated
This is typically the only consistent symptom. Because EVAP systems operate in the background, many drivers won’t notice anything else until the warning appears.

✔ Fuel smell around the vehicle
A strong fuel odor, especially near the rear of the vehicle, often indicates vapors escaping due to a disconnected hose or faulty component.

✔ Rough idle (in some cases)
If the purge valve is stuck open, excess vapor can enter the engine, disrupting the air-fuel mixture and causing unstable idle conditions.

✔ Hard starting after refueling
This is a classic sign of EVAP issues. The engine may struggle to start because excess fuel vapor floods the intake.

✔ Slight drop in fuel economy
While usually minimal, inefficiencies in vapor recovery can slightly impact overall fuel usage.

Causes

• Loose or faulty gas cap (most common)
The simplest and most overlooked cause. If the gas cap isn’t sealing properly, the system cannot maintain pressure.

• Faulty N80 purge valve
Very common on Audi vehicles. A stuck-open purge valve allows vapors to flow incorrectly, triggering leak detection.

• Cracked or disconnected EVAP hoses
Audi uses plastic hoses that can become brittle over time. Even a small crack can act like a “large leak” to the system.

• Faulty vent valve
If the vent valve doesn’t close properly, the system cannot seal during testing.

• Charcoal canister or fuel system leaks
Physical damage or wear in the canister or tank components can cause significant vapor leaks.

Step-by-Step Diagnosis

1. Check the gas cap first
Tighten it until it clicks. If it looks worn or cracked, replace it — this alone fixes a large percentage of P0455 cases.

2. Reset the code and retest
Clear the code and drive for a few cycles. If it doesn’t return, the issue was likely temporary or cap-related.

3. Inspect EVAP hoses visually
Look for obvious cracks, disconnections, or brittle lines, especially near the engine bay.

4. Test the N80 purge valve
Listen for clicking during operation or test electrically. A stuck valve is one of the most common Audi-specific failures.

5. Check vent valve operation
Ensure it properly seals when commanded by the ECU.

6. Perform a smoke test
This is the fastest and most accurate way to find large leaks. Smoke escaping will pinpoint the exact failure location.

How to Fix

  • ✔ Tighten or replace gas cap
  • ✔ Replace N80 purge valve
  • ✔ Repair EVAP hoses
  • ✔ Replace vent valve
  • ✔ Repair canister or fuel system leaks

💡 Pro Tip

If everything looks fine, you may actually be dealing with a smaller EVAP leak that’s harder to detect. These require a smoke test and often trigger codes like P0442.
