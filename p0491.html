P0491

Secondary Air Injection System Insufficient Flow (Bank 1)

Difficulty
Hard
Repair Time
2–6 hours
Cost
$0–$1200+
Severity
Medium

Why This Matters

P0491 is one of those Audi codes that people underestimate because the car often still drives fine. In reality, this system is critical during cold starts. The secondary air injection (SAI) system pumps fresh air into the exhaust to help burn off emissions quickly. When airflow is restricted, the ECU detects it immediately and throws this code.

The real issue is what causes the restriction. On many Audi engines — especially the 3.0T and V8 — carbon buildup inside the cylinder head air passages slowly chokes off airflow. I’ve seen ports almost completely blocked. At that point, no pump or valve replacement will fix it until those passages are physically cleaned.

Symptoms

  • ✔ Check engine light (usually on cold start)
  • ✔ Loud whining or hissing from SAI pump on startup
  • ✔ Rough or slightly unstable cold idle
  • ✔ Sluggish acceleration in some cases

Most drivers notice this during cold starts. The SAI pump kicks on for about 30–90 seconds, and if something is wrong, it often sounds louder than normal. In some cases, it almost sounds like a vacuum leak or a struggling electric motor.

Once the engine warms up, symptoms usually disappear — which is why this problem gets ignored. But the code will keep coming back until the airflow issue is resolved.

Causes

  • • Carbon buildup blocking cylinder head SAI ports (MOST COMMON)
  • • Failed or stuck combi (check) valve
  • • Vacuum leaks affecting valve operation
  • • Weak or failing secondary air injection pump

In real-world diagnostics, carbon buildup is the main culprit — not the pump. Many people replace the SAI pump first and waste money because the airflow is still blocked downstream.

The combi valve is another failure point. If it sticks open, hot exhaust gases can travel backward and damage the pump. If it stays closed, airflow never reaches the exhaust, triggering the code.

How to Fix

  • ✔ Clean carbon from cylinder head SAI ports (pressure wash method)
  • ✔ Replace faulty combi valves
  • ✔ Inspect and repair vacuum lines
  • ✔ Replace damaged SAI pump if needed

The correct fix depends on airflow testing. If airflow is low or nonexistent, the cylinder head ports must be cleaned. This is often done using specialized tools or pressure washing methods after removing components.

If airflow is present but inconsistent, focus on the combi valves and vacuum system. These control when air is allowed into the exhaust and are common failure points.

Only replace the pump if it’s confirmed weak or damaged. Otherwise, you’ll fix nothing and still have the code.

💡 Pro Tip

If you have both P0491 and P0492, don’t replace parts yet — it’s almost always carbon blockage in both banks. I’ve seen people replace pumps, valves, and hoses, only to end up doing the carbon cleaning anyway.

Also check for Audi TSBs or extended warranties. Some models had coverage for this exact issue up to 10 years / 80,000 miles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drive with P0491?

Yes, but it will keep triggering the check engine light and emissions tests will fail.

What causes P0491 on Audi?

Most commonly carbon buildup blocking the SAI ports inside the cylinder head.

Is P0491 expensive to fix?

It can be free (DIY cleaning) or over $1000 depending on labor.
