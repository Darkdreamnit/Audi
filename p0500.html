P0500

Vehicle Speed Sensor 'A' Malfunction

Intermediate

1–3 hrs

High

Deep Dive

The P0500 code on Audi vehicles indicates that the ECU is not receiving a proper speed signal. In real-world cases, this often shows up as a completely dead speedometer or erratic transmission behavior. On many Audi models, the vehicle speed signal doesn’t always come from a single dedicated sensor—it’s often calculated using ABS wheel speed sensors. This means the issue can come from multiple places, including the ABS module, wiring, or even a damaged tone ring.

Symptoms

✔ Speedometer not working or reading incorrectly
A complete loss of speedometer function is one of the most obvious signs. This occurs when the ECU receives no usable speed signal at all.

✔ Harsh or erratic shifting
Transmission behavior depends on speed input. Without it, shifting becomes unpredictable or aggressive.

✔ Limp mode activation
The vehicle may restrict gear usage to protect internal components.

✔ ABS or traction control warning lights
Because these systems share speed data, they often trigger warnings alongside this code.

✔ Cruise control failure
Cruise control relies on accurate speed readings and will stop working.

✔ Reduced performance
The vehicle may feel sluggish or limited due to incorrect system calculations.

Causes

• Faulty vehicle speed sensor
The most direct cause, where the sensor fails to send data entirely.

• Wiring damage
Broken or corroded wires interrupt signal transmission.

• Faulty ABS wheel speed sensor
Very common on Audi vehicles due to shared data systems.

• ABS module failure
The module processing speed signals may malfunction.

• Damaged tone ring
Debris or cracks disrupt signal generation.

• ECU issue (rare)
The ECU may fail to interpret signals properly.

Step-by-Step Diagnosis

1. Scan for codes
Identify P0500 and related ABS faults.

2. Check speedometer behavior
Dead vs intermittent helps isolate cause.

3. Inspect wiring
Look for visible damage.

4. Test sensor
Verify signal output.

5. Scan ABS module
Check for wheel sensor faults.

6. Inspect tone ring
Look for cracks or debris.

How to Fix

  • ✔ Replace faulty speed sensor
  • ✔ Repair wiring
  • ✔ Replace ABS sensor
  • ✔ Clean tone ring
  • ✔ Replace ABS module if needed
