P0700

Transmission Control System Malfunction

Difficulty
Intermediate to Advanced
Repair Time
1–5 hours
Cost
$100–$3000+
Severity
High

Why This Matters

The P0700 code is essentially a warning flag from your vehicle’s transmission system. While it doesn’t point to a specific failure on its own, it means the Transmission Control Module (TCM) has detected something wrong. Ignoring this can lead to worsening drivability issues like harsh shifting or the car entering limp mode, and in the worst cases, long-term transmission damage.

Symptoms

✔ Check engine light or transmission warning
This is usually the first indicator. In many Audi vehicles, you may also see a transmission-specific warning message on the dashboard, signaling that the system has detected an internal fault.

✔ Hard, delayed, or jerky shifting
If the transmission isn’t shifting smoothly, it’s often due to solenoids or pressure control issues. You may feel the car hesitate before shifting or slam into gear.

✔ Limp mode (limited gears)
The vehicle may restrict itself to one or two gears to protect the transmission. This is a safety feature designed to prevent further damage.

✔ Sluggish acceleration
When the transmission isn’t operating properly, power delivery becomes inconsistent. This can make the car feel slow or unresponsive.

Causes

• Low or dirty transmission fluid
One of the most common causes. Dirty or low fluid affects hydraulic pressure, which is critical for proper shifting and operation.

• Faulty shift solenoids
Solenoids control fluid flow inside the transmission. When they fail, the transmission may shift incorrectly or not at all.

• Wiring or electrical issues
Damaged wiring or poor connections can interrupt communication between the TCM and transmission components.

• Speed sensor failures
Input and output speed sensors help determine shift timing. If they fail, the transmission can behave unpredictably.

• Valve body problems
The valve body directs fluid to different parts of the transmission. Issues here can cause widespread shifting problems.

• Faulty TCM
In some cases, the control module itself may fail or require reprogramming.

Step-by-Step Diagnosis

1. Scan the transmission module (TCM)
This is the most important step. P0700 alone is not enough — you must retrieve the actual transmission codes stored in the TCM.

2. Record all related codes
Codes like P0715 or P0730 will point you toward the real issue, such as sensor or gear ratio problems.

3. Check transmission fluid condition
Inspect the fluid level and color. Burnt or dark fluid is a strong indicator of internal wear or overheating.

4. Inspect wiring and connectors
Look for damaged wires, corrosion, or loose connectors that could disrupt signals.

5. Test solenoids and sensors
Using a scan tool or multimeter, verify that components are functioning within specifications.

6. Verify TCM power and ground
Ensure the module is receiving proper voltage and connections before assuming it has failed.

How to Fix

  • ✔ Replace transmission fluid
  • ✔ Replace solenoids
  • ✔ Repair wiring
  • ✔ Replace sensors
  • ✔ Repair valve body
  • ✔ Replace or reprogram TCM

⚠️ Real-World Tip

Don’t panic — P0700 is just a gateway code. Always scan the TCM before replacing parts. Many Audi owners fix this with simple maintenance rather than major repairs.
